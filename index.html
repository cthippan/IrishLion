Our Story

Effigy vessels featuring human or animal features are one of the hallmarks of the Mississippian culture that flourished in the Southeastern and Midwestern United States between approximately 1000 to 1500 ACE. Effigy elements were often applied or modeled on all sides of a vessel, including the rim and base. Although many types of artifacts could benefit from 3D modeling, the ability to view effigy vessels in a 3D environment is particularly useful for this reason. These models would be useful for academic research, public outreach, and to provide a baseline for future conservation assessments of the physical artifact.
