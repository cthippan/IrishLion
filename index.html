Our Story

  • Humble Beginnings

    The Irish Lion Restaurant & Pub, originally a bar and hotel, was built in 1882 by Mr. Zimri McCollough, an early settler of the county. He was a successful livery man and constructed the current building to cater to the public and passengers of the former Monon Train Depot across the street. The Worley Tavern and the Hotel Bundy European opened upstairs. In the following 96 years, the main level has been predominantly a tavern with billiard tables, in addition to providing lunch and tobacco products.

  • Prohibition

    However the onset of Prohibition changed the business’s emphasis to only billiards and tobacco. A painted mural, advertising "Lovel Burch Hotel Bundy European," was located on the east side of the building. It has since weathered away and only some fragments of a “Mail Pouch Tobacco” advertisement, added much later remain.

  • Transition

    From 1935 to 1957, an array of taverns and billiard establishments came and went—a dark era for the building. From 1957 to 1968, the main level housed Hinkle’s Lunch Restaurant and the upstairs was vacant. In 1969, The Kirkwood Bar occupied the main level. It closed its doors in 1978. In 1979, after a brief vacancy, the McConnaughy family purchased the building and embarked upon what would turn out to be an extensive restoration process. The present Irish Lion Restaurant and Pub opened its doors in 1982.

  • Renovations

    The decorative double doors and the surrounding woodwork at the front entrance are original to the building. The stained glass of that same period was acquired in Indianapolis, having been added in 1982. The woodwork on the main floor is mostly oak while the upstairs is American Gumwood. The decorative wall tile in the vestibule is original and was duplicated on the other side.

  • The Back Bar

    TThe mahogany back bar in the main pub is made by Brunswick, the well-known manufacturer of billiard tables. This style was made from the late 1860s to the 1880s. The back bar was previously located in a pub in Indianapolis, at the corner of East St. and Washington. There are two sections of the downstairs front. The base of the bar with the scrolled areas came from Vincennes, Indiana and dates to the 1850s. The bar top and two end sections came from English, Indiana and were made later, around 1880.
